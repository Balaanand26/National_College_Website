B.Sc. Fashion Design

B.Sc. Fashion Design

Our Fashion Design program is a great starting point to get an overview of the fashion industry before committing to the full 3-year Bachelor's Degree Program or Diploma Program.

Perhaps you are considering a career change after years of working in another field, or you have already studied another major but want to add new skills to your skill set or profile.

You can learn so much from the degree and diploma programs, from basic drawing and computer illustration skills to pattern cutting and garment construction. You can then decide whether to continue with the Bachelor’s program or venture into the fashion world.

Our Fashion Design program aims to give students a connection to all facets of the fashion industry, from product design and development to marketing and management.

The mission of our Fashion Design program is to prepare students for careers in the fashion industry or to continue postgraduate studies. This is achieved by providing students with a learning environment that accelerates creativity, modern business techniques, research, and global perception.

Accreditation

Fashion Design Courses

B.Sc. Fashion Design

This program is designed to help students gain in-depth knowledge about the world of fashion. Students learn the creative process, cultural influences on design, production, marketing, communication, and more. This course focuses on fabric choice and care before moving on to advanced sewing skills such as decorations and embroidery. It also emphasizes constructing patterns for specific garments. Students learn the basics of flat and garment detail drawing, illustrations using Adobe Illustrator, and building a styling portfolio.

The B.Sc. Fashion Design program combines fashion studies with intensive studio practice. You will refine your own creative vision as you meet the challenge of designing new clothing and accessories for existing fashion brands. B.Sc. Fashion Design professionals may receive offers from both government and private design companies. They may work in positions such as fashion stylist, fashion coordinator, fashion consultant, colorist, apparel merchandiser, fashion journalist, fashion photographer, freelancer, costume designer, technical designer, and visual merchandiser. You will gain a deep understanding of the fashion industry and be well-prepared for an exciting career within it.

Significance Of Fashion Design

The employment of fashion designers is projected to grow by 33 percent over the next ten years, although this rate is slower than the average for all occupations.

A career in fashion design is right for you if you are talented and exude style in everything you do. You must also possess originality and creativity, along with a passion for making people look good.

Most apparel continues to be produced internationally. As a result, employment of fashion designers in the apparel manufacturing industry is projected to decline by about 33 percent over the forecasted period, slowing the overall employment growth of fashion designers.

However, employment of fashion designers in the retail trade industry is projected to grow by about 22 percent over the forecasted period. Retailers are selling more fashion-inspired clothing, which increases the demand for fashion designers to create clothing and accessories for the mass market.
